Sedan & Hatchback

A sedan is a passenger car with a separate trunk (boot) for luggage. It typically has four doors and a traditional three-box design (engine, cabin, and trunk).

Uses

  • Ideal for families or individuals needing a comfortable ride.
  • Commonly used for daily commuting and long-distance travel due to good ride quality and spacious interiors.

    • Pick-ups & SUVs

    SUVs (Sport Utility Vehicles) and Pick-up Trucks are larger, rugged vehicles designed for utility, space, and performance. Both typically offer higher ground clearance and may include all-wheel or four-wheel drive options. The main difference: SUVs are enclosed and focused on passenger comfort, while pick-ups have an open cargo bed for hauling..

    Uses:

  • Ideal for carrying heavy loads, tools, or equipment.
  • Commonly used in construction, farming, and by outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Good for road trips, off-road driving, and bad weather conditions.

    • Vans

    Vans and Multi-Purpose Vehicles (MPVs) are both types of vehicles designed for carrying people and cargo, but they differ in size and design. Vans are typically larger and boxier, with a high roofline, designed to maximize interior space for freight and/or passengers. MPVs are generally smaller and more aerodynamic, with a focus on passenger comfort and a more car-like driving experience.
